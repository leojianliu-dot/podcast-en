Moldova sits in Eastern Europe, landlocked between Romania and Ukraine. It's about 34,000 square kilometers — roughly the size of Maryland. Population: just 2.4 million. Capital: Chisinau. Its GDP per capita is the lowest in Europe. This is a country the world barely notices.

But small doesn't mean without a story. Moldova and Romania share one of the most unusual relationships in Europe. Historically, most of modern Moldova was part of the Principality of Moldavia. It was annexed by the Russian Empire in 1812, briefly reunited with Romania between the World Wars, then absorbed into the Soviet Union. Independence came in 1991 when the USSR collapsed.

So Moldova's national identity has been pulled in two directions ever since — west toward its Romanian roots and European identity, or east toward its traditional ties with Russia.
