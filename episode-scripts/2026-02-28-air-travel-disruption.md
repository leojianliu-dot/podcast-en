If your flight has been cancelled, you are entitled to a full refund. Period. Airlines must offer you either a ticket on an alternative flight or your money back. This is true regardless of which airline or which country you booked from.

If you're departing from an EU or EEA airport, you have additional protections under EU261 Duty of Care regulations. Airlines are required to provide meals, hotel accommodation, and transportation until your rebooked flight departs — regardless of the cause of the cancellation.

In Israel, the Tibi Law specifically requires airlines to offer either an alternative flight or a full monetary refund, plus assistance services including hotels, meals, and transport.

However — and this is important — airlines are generally exempt from paying additional compensation beyond the refund when the disruption is caused by extraordinary circumstances beyond their control, like a military conflict. So you'll get your money back or a new flight, but probably not the extra cash compensation you'd normally get for a garden-variety delay or cancellation.
