Eurovision, or the Eurovision Song Contest, is the world's largest non-sporting live TV event. It happens every May. Around 35 to 40 countries each send one artist with an original song to compete. Last year's contest drew over 160 million viewers worldwide.

The contest dates back to 1956. Less than a decade after World War Two, European countries were still rebuilding from the rubble. Television was a brand new technology, and the European Broadcasting Union wanted to test whether they could link multiple countries together for a simultaneous live broadcast. So someone suggested — why not do a singing contest? The first edition had just seven countries and was held in Switzerland. Seventy years later, it's become a massive cultural phenomenon.

How important is Eurovision? Let me give you a few names. ABBA — one of the best-selling music groups of all time — won Eurovision in 1974 with Waterloo. The rules are simple. Each song can't exceed three minutes. It must be original. It must be performed live. Rankings are decided by a fifty-fifty split between public televoting and professional juries. The winning country gets to host the following year.
